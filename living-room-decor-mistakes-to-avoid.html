5 Decor Mistakes That Make Your Living Room Look Cheap (And How I Fixed Them)

C
By Chic Living Spaces Editorial • July 19, 2026

Let’s be honest: decorating a living room is hard. When I moved into my first place, I spent hours scrolling through Pinterest, buying things I thought looked beautiful. But when I put them all together? My living room looked cluttered, disconnected, and surprisingly... cheap.

I couldn't figure out why my space didn't look like the magazines, even though I had spent good money on furniture. After consulting with a few interior designer friends and doing a lot of trial and error, I realized I was making five fundamental decorating mistakes.

The good news? You don’t need a massive budget to fix them. Small tweaks can completely transform the aesthetic of your room. Here are the mistakes I made, and the exact steps I took to fix them.

1. The "Postage Stamp" Rug

This was my biggest offense. I bought a 5x8 rug for a massive living room because it was cheaper. The result? It looked like a tiny island floating in the middle of my floor, making the entire room feel smaller and disjointed.

MY FIX: SIZE UP THE RUG

I sized up to an 8x10 rug. The golden rule: The front legs of all your major seating furniture (sofa and chairs) should rest on the rug. If you have a beautiful small rug you absolutely love, layer it over a larger, inexpensive jute or sisal rug!

"A rug that is too small makes the room look like it shrunk in the wash. Size up, always."

2. Hanging Curtains Too Low and Tight

I used to hang my curtain rod right above the window frame and buy panels that just barely reached the floor. It made my ceilings feel low and the windows look tiny.

MY FIX: HIGH AND WIDE

I moved the curtain rod up—almost to the ceiling line (or at least 4-6 inches above the window frame). I also extended the rod 8-12 inches wider than the window on each side. Now, when the curtains are open, they frame the window rather than blocking the light. Result: My standard 8-foot ceilings suddenly looked 10 feet high!

3. Buying the "Matching Set"

When you walk into a big-box furniture store, it’s so tempting to buy the matching sofa, loveseat, and armchair bundle. I did this. My living room ended up looking like a showroom catalog without any soul or personality.

MY FIX: MIX AND MATCH

I sold the matching loveseat on Facebook Marketplace and replaced it with two contrasting accent chairs. Mixing textures—like a fabric sofa, a leather chair, and a wood coffee table—creates a curated, collected-over-time look. It instantly elevated the space from "store-bought" to "designer-styled."

4. Ignoring Art Scale and Placement

Have you ever seen a tiny 8x10 photo frame hanging alone above a massive 84-inch sofa? That was me. It throws off the entire balance of the room. I also used to hang my art way too high, almost touching the ceiling.

MY FIX: EYE LEVEL RULE

Art should be hung at eye level (about 57 to 60 inches from the floor to the center of the art). When hanging art over furniture, the art (or gallery wall) should fill about two-thirds to three-quarters of the width of the furniture below it. If you can't afford massive art, a gallery wall or a large textured canvas is a great budget-friendly alternative.

5. Relying Exclusively on the "Big Light"

For years, my only light source was the harsh overhead fixture. At night, my living room felt like a hospital waiting room—cold and unwelcoming.

MY FIX: LAYERED LIGHTING

I embraced layered lighting. I added a tall arched floor lamp in the corner, a small table lamp on the side table, and even some warm LED strip lighting behind the TV. Turning off the "big light" and relying on warm, ambient lamps completely transformed the mood of my home. It now feels cozy, intimate, and chic.

Final Thoughts

Decorating is a journey, and making mistakes is just part of figuring out your personal style. By fixing these five simple things—sizing up your rug, raising your curtains, mixing furniture, scaling your art, and layering your lighting—you can make even the most budget-friendly living room look incredibly high-end.
